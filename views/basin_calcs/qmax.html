Analysis Description
Q = f(area)

The simpliest Rainfall - Runoff model has published in 1851 by Thomas James Mulvaney using a single simple equation : Qp = C*A*R and is known widely as "rational method" (where, C:empirical coeffient, A: catchment area, R: rainfall intensity). It is most common, hydrologist's during their first hydrological investigation of a catchment are dealing with limited data. Therefore varius simple Rainfall-Runoff empirical models exist using as one parameter the catchment area and estimate Qpeak or Qmax.(soon more Detailed Text)

Waterdrops uses:

  • Friedrich = 24.12 * A0.516,
  • Klement-Wunderlich = 5.5 * A 5/6 ,
  • Wundt = 13.8 * A0.6,
  • Valentini = (30 / A1/2)* A, where A is catchment area.
